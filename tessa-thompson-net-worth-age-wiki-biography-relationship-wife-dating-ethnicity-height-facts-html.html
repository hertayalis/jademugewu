Tessa Thompson, born on October 3, 1983, in Los Angeles, California, is a versatile American actress and singer. Her journey into the world of acting began during her time at Santa Monica College, where she participated in productions by the Los Angeles Women’s Shakespeare Company. Her early performances in plays like Romeo and Juliet and The Tempest earned her a nomination for a NAACP Theater Award.

Thompson’s breakthrough came with significant roles in Tyler Perry’s For Colored Girls and the indie drama Mississippi Damned in 2009. She gained positive reviews for her performances in Dear White People and Selma in 2014, portraying civil rights activist Diane Nash.
