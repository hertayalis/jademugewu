The Bear Power Animal as a Teacher and Healer

For many cultures around the world, the bear is closely connected with the shamanic tradition. For example, among the Inuit of Northern America, this animal is associated with the Shamanic tradition and women.

Also, in Siberia, the word ‘bear’ signifies a woman shaman.

In some communities, hunters welcomed being eaten by bears. Tradition has it that such hunters would reincarnate as shamans.

Communities living around the polar region consider the polar bear a wise teacher. They associate its ability to survive under harsh conditions with wisdom.

The association of the bear with shamans points to the healing properties of this spirit animal. It means that you are able to heal yourself and to extend this healing to those around you.

When this spirit animal comes into your life, you’ll experience a unique youthful rejuvenation. You’ll be uplifted spiritually, emotionally, and physically.

It’s important that you identify correctly the areas of your life that need healing. Of course, you can summon the bear spirit animal to provide you with the right energy to deal with your healing needs more decisively.

Of all the totems, the bear spirit is the most worshipped in the ancient world. Archeological evidence in Europe, South America, and Northern America show that the bear was held in high regard.

The remains of bear skulls and bones have been found alongside human remains in these areas.

History has it that Vikings used to wear bear skins in the battlefront. This would provide them with the courage they need to face their enemies.

Also, their enemies would be scared by the ferocious look of the Vikings in bearskin clothes.

The ancient Greeks and Romans associated this animal with the Goddess Artemis and Diana. These two deities are associated with fertility.

On the other hand, the Celts used the bear symbol to represent the sun.
