What is TCF Bank?

TCF Bank is a regional bank based in Minnesota. They have over 700 branches in the Midwest and offer a full range of banking services, from checking and savings accounts to loans and investment products.

The History of TCF Bank

TCF Bank was founded in 1923 as the Twin City Federal Savings and Loan Association. It changed its name to TCF Financial Corporation in 1979 and then to TCF Bank in 1987. In recent years, the bank has expanded beyond its Midwestern roots and now has branches in Arizona, Colorado, Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Ohio, and Wisconsin.

The Merging of TCF Bank and Huntington Bank

In 2015, TCF Bank merged with Huntington Bank. This was a strategic move by both banks, as they hoped to create a stronger bank that could compete better in the current market. 

However, the merger did not go smoothly for customers. They found themselves having to deal with two different customer service lines and systems. This was confusing and frustrating for many people, as they had to learn how to use two different websites and call centers. 

In addition, there were some problems with the way information was transferred between the two banks, which resulted in incorrect charges and account balances.

Customer Service Issues After the Merger

After the merger took place, customer service at both TCF Bank and Huntington Bank was less than satisfactory. Customers complained about long wait times on the phone and poor customer service from representatives. 

In addition, there were numerous complaints about incorrect charges and account balances after information was transferred between the two banks. Many customers felt that they were not getting the help they needed from either bank.

Most banks are closed on weekends. However, TCF is open for customers on Saturdays who may not have time during the week. TFC Bank are open from 9 am-1 pm on Saturdays. The hours for each day are as follows:

What To Do If The Bank Is Closed?

It can be a disaster when you need to get to a bank, but it’s closed. 

Here are a few tips on what to do:

  • Try using the ATM: Most banks have ATMs that are available 24/7. You will be able to withdraw cash, but you may be charged a fee.
  • Use online or mobile banking: If you have an account with TCF Bank, you should be able to use these. This will allow you to check your account balance, transfer money, and make payments.
  • Call the customer service number: If you need to speak to someone about your account, you can call the customer service number. They may not be able to help with everything, though.
  • Visit a different bank: If you need to cash a check or make a deposit, you can try visiting a different bank. Most banks will be able to help you out, even if you don’t have an account with them.
  • Cash your check somewhere else: There are a few places that will cash your check even if you don’t have an account with them. These places include grocery stores, check-cashing stores, and Western Union. You can even use apps these days!

Tips On Finding A New Bank 

When you’re shopping for a new bank, it’s important to compare your options and find the right fit for you. 

Here are a few tips when you’re shopping for a bank:

  • Consider your needs: What do you need from a bank? Do you need a checking account, savings account, or both? Are you looking for a bank with branches near you?
  • Compare fees: Banks charge fees for various services. Compare the fees charged by different banks to see which one is the most affordable.
  • Read reviews: Before you choose a bank, be sure to read reviews from other customers. This will give you an idea of what to expect in terms of customer service and fees.
  • Check the hours of your local branches: Make sure the bank you choose has branches that are open when you need them to be.
  • Speak to your friends and family: Ask if they have any recommendations for banks. They may be able to give you some good advice or warn you about bad experiences they’ve had.
  • Research customer satisfaction ratings: Customer satisfaction ratings can give you a good idea of how satisfied customers are with a particular bank.
